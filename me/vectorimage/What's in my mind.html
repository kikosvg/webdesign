Thought 1 about traveling

I really like moments when I'm not doing anything. Like walking out of the airport, or speeding through the night in California in a car. These moments have no gradient, they have the quality of a cutscene. You watch the world spinning at a constant speed beneath your feet. There's nothing to do, and you quietly feel your anticipation.

I went on a trip to Mexico last week. We stayed on the tropical island of Cancun. The main part of the island is a resort. It's very similar to the island in the game “Bulletproof 2”: the whole island is divided into several districts connected by small paths, and the districts are sometimes open and sometimes closed. The island is inhabited by people who make a living raising tourists. The color of the island is the color of the sea. The air and drinking water both smell of the sea. The hurricane has swept the sea water onto the shore, and the corridors are covered in puddles of water of all depths. We shuffle along on the damp ground, like little crabs crawling on the beach at low tide. The inhabitants are lazy. They know each other and are happy every day. They don't care when they see the tourists being blown in from the sea and blown back out again. They cook the cactus or cut it up and make juice. When they feel like it, they learn a little of the tourists' language. The island is very hot and humid, and it rains a lot. Sometimes it only rains for twenty seconds, like in a magic-realist novel. The inhabitants often drive around in their little tourist cars to catch tourists. When it rains, they lower and lock the little tourist car's rain curtain. The locked little tourist car looks like a shell with its mouth closed in the tide.
